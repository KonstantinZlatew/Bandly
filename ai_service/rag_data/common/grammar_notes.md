# IELTS Grammar Range and Accuracy – Band Descriptors

## Band 9 – Grammar
- Consistent and accurate use of a wide range of complex sentence structures.
- Frequent use of:
  - Subordinate clauses
  - Conditionals (including mixed conditionals)
  - Passive voice where appropriate
  - Inversion and emphasis structures
- Errors are extremely rare and insignificant.
- Grammar enhances meaning and clarity, never limits expression.

## Band 8 – Grammar
- Uses a wide range of complex sentence forms naturally.
- Minor errors may occur, but they do not reduce clarity.
- Good control of:
  - Relative clauses
  - Complex verb tenses
  - Modality
- Sentence variety is clear and intentional.

## Band 7 – Grammar
- Uses a mix of simple and complex sentence forms.
- Complex sentences are generally accurate but may contain noticeable errors.
- Errors do not impede communication.
- Some repetition of sentence patterns may be present.

## Band 6 – Grammar
- Uses both simple and some complex sentence structures.
- Errors in complex sentences are common.
- Grammar mistakes may reduce precision but meaning is usually clear.
- Limited control of complex structures.

## Band 5 – Grammar
- Relies mainly on simple sentence forms.
- Frequent grammatical errors, including:
  - Verb tense mistakes
  - Subject-verb disagreement
- Errors may cause misunderstanding.
- Very limited sentence variety.

## Band 4 and below – Grammar
- Persistent grammatical errors dominate the text.
- Very limited control of sentence structure.
- Meaning is often unclear due to grammar issues.

## Common problem areas:
- Articles (a, an, the)
- Subject–verb agreement
- Run-on sentences
- Incorrect conditionals
- Punctuation misuse
