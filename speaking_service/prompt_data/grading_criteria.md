# IELTS SPEAKING GRADING CRITERIA

---

## Band 4
**Fluency and Coherence:**  
- Speech is slow and often hesitant  
- Pauses and self-corrections disrupt communication  
- Limited ability to link ideas

**Lexical Resource:**  
- Very limited vocabulary  
- Frequent repetition of simple words  
- Cannot paraphrase effectively

**Grammar:**  
- Frequent errors in simple sentence structures  
- Very few complex structures attempted  
- Errors often cause misunderstandings

**Pronunciation:**  
- Often difficult to understand  
- Mispronunciation is common  
- Intonation and rhythm largely unnatural

---

## Band 5
**Fluency and Coherence:**  
- Speech is slow with frequent pauses  
- Coherence is sometimes maintained  
- Repetition common, ideas occasionally unclear

**Lexical Resource:**  
- Limited vocabulary for familiar topics  
- Attempts at less common words often fail  
- Paraphrasing is rare and awkward

**Grammar:**  
- Simple sentences mostly correct  
- Complex sentences rarely attempted  
- Errors sometimes impede understanding

**Pronunciation:**  
- Generally understandable, though effort required  
- Mispronunciations noticeable  
- Intonation inconsistent

---

## Band 6
**Fluency and Coherence:**  
- Speaks at length on familiar topics  
- May lose coherence occasionally  
- Hesitation is noticeable but meaning is clear

**Lexical Resource:**  
- Sufficient vocabulary to discuss familiar topics  
- Limited ability to use less common words  
- Some repetition

**Grammar:**  
- Mix of simple and complex sentence structures  
- Frequent errors with complex structures  
- Errors do not usually impede communication

**Pronunciation:**  
- Generally intelligible  
- Occasional mispronunciation  
- Accent may affect clarity slightly

---

## Band 7
**Fluency and Coherence:**  
- Speaks at length without noticeable effort  
- Rare loss of coherence  
- Hesitation is natural, meaning clear

**Lexical Resource:**  
- Uses vocabulary flexibly to discuss topics  
- Can paraphrase effectively  
- Occasional awkward word choice

**Grammar:**  
- Uses a range of complex structures accurately  
- Some minor errors, but meaning is unaffected  
- Sentence structures are varied

**Pronunciation:**  
- Clear pronunciation throughout  
- Minor lapses do not affect understanding  
- Intonation is appropriate

---

## Band 8
**Fluency and Coherence:**  
- Speaks fluently with only occasional repetition or self-correction  
- Rarely loses coherence  
- Speech flows naturally

**Lexical Resource:**  
- Uses a wide range of vocabulary readily  
- Can express precise meaning  
- Uses idiomatic language appropriately

**Grammar:**  
- Produces error-free sentences frequently  
- Complex sentence structures consistently accurate  
- Very few noticeable mistakes

**Pronunciation:**  
- Easy to understand throughout  
- Natural rhythm and intonation  
- Only occasional lapses

---

## Band 9
**Fluency and Coherence:**  
- Speaks fluently with complete flexibility  
- Ideas flow naturally with sophisticated discourse markers  
- No hesitation, repetition, or loss of coherence

**Lexical Resource:**  
- Uses a full range of vocabulary precisely  
- Expresses nuances of meaning effortlessly  
- Idiomatic expressions are accurate and natural

**Grammar:**  
- Completely accurate use of complex structures  
- No errors affecting communication  
- Wide variety of sentence forms consistently used

**Pronunciation:**  
- Pronunciation is completely natural and effortless  
- Intonation, stress, and rhythm fully accurate  
- Easily understood in all situations
